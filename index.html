1) Introduction

Welcome to GenAEX (the “App”). We value your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use the App in the United States. If you do not agree with the terms in this Policy, please do not use the App.

2) Scope & Audience

This Policy applies to personal information processed by GenAEX in connection with your use of the App and related services. It does not apply to third-party services that are not owned or controlled by us.

3) Information We Collect

A. Information You Provide

  • Account & Profile: Name or nickname, email address, and preferences (if you choose to create an account or provide them).
  • Expense Records: Amounts, categories, payment method labels, notes, dates, tags, and other personal budgeting details you enter.
  • Support & Communication: Information you include when contacting us (e.g., requests, attachments).

B. Information Collected Automatically

  • Device & App Data: Device model, operating system version, app version, language, settings, and performance diagnostics.
  • Usage Data: Features you use, screens viewed, and in-app events to help us improve stability and design.
  • Approximate Location: Only if you grant permission, for features such as currency or locale suggestions. We do not collect precise (GPS-level) location unless explicitly required by a feature you enable.

C. Information from Third Parties

  • App Store Providers: Purchase history related to subscriptions or in-app purchases (no full payment card details are received by us).
  • Analytics/Crash Services: Aggregated or pseudonymous information to improve app quality.

D. Sensitive Information

The App is designed for personal expense tracking. Please avoid entering sensitive personal information (e.g., Social Security numbers, health records) into free-form fields. We do not intentionally collect such information.

4) How We Use Information

  • Provide, personalize, and maintain the App and your expense records.
  • Process purchases and manage subscriptions.
  • Analyze usage to improve performance, reliability, and user experience.
  • Communicate with you about updates, security alerts, and support messages.
  • Detect, prevent, and address technical issues or potential misuse.
  • Comply with legal obligations and enforce our terms.

5) Legal Bases (where applicable)

When a legal basis is required (e.g., if using the App from a jurisdiction that requires it), we rely on: (i) performance of a contract (providing the App), (ii) legitimate interests (e.g., to improve and secure the App), (iii) consent (where you grant permissions), and/or (iv) compliance with legal obligations.

6) How We Share Information

We do not sell or “share” (as defined under California law) your personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising. We may disclose information to:

  • Service Providers: Vendors who process data on our behalf (e.g., analytics, crash reporting, cloud storage, customer support). They are required to use information only as instructed.
  • Payment Processors & App Stores: For in-app purchases and subscription management. We do not receive full payment card numbers.
  • Legal & Safety: If required by law or to protect rights, safety, and integrity of our users, the public, or the App.
  • Business Transfers: In connection with a merger, acquisition, or asset sale, subject to this Policy’s protections.

7) Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to provide the App, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements. You may request deletion of your account or data at any time (see Contact Us).

8) Security

We employ administrative, technical, and organizational measures designed to protect personal information. However, no method of transmission or storage is completely secure; we cannot guarantee absolute security.

9) Children’s Privacy

The App is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided personal information, please contact us and we will take steps to delete it.

10) U.S. State Privacy Rights (including California)

Depending on where you live, you may have rights regarding your personal information. For example, if you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (as amended by the CPRA) grants certain rights. Similar rights may exist in states such as Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, and Utah. These may include:

  • Right to Know/Access the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected.
  • Right to Delete personal information, subject to exceptions.
  • Right to Correct inaccurate personal information.
  • Right to Opt-Out of the sale or sharing of personal information (we do not sell or share for cross-context behavioral advertising).
  • Right to Limit use of sensitive personal information (we do not use sensitive information for inferring characteristics).
  • Right to Non-Discrimination for exercising privacy rights.

How to Exercise Your Rights

You can submit a request by emailing us at ludvikcernosek@gmail.com. To protect your privacy, we may verify your identity before acting on your request. You may also authorize an agent to submit a request on your behalf, subject to verification.
